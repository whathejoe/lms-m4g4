Lesson 50: Describing Triangles and Quadrilaterals Explore and Discover!

Mr. Tabilang bought a cartolina for his Math class. He needs two triangles of the same size without wasting any parts of the cartolina. How will he divide it?

What shapes are formed?

A triangle is a polygon with 3 slides and 3 angles.

A polygon. is a closed figure made up of several line segments that are joined together.

The line segments are called sides and do not cross each other. There are exactly two sides that meet at a point.

A quadrilateral is a polygon with 4 sides and 4 angles.
